The Professional’s Blueprint: What is Forex and How the Market Actually Moves

The foreign exchange (Forex) market is the largest and most liquid financial market in the world, with an average daily turnover exceeding $9.6 trillion. It operates as a decentralized global network where traders buy and sell currency pairs such as EUR/USD to profit from fluctuations in exchange rates.

How the Forex Market Works

Unlike stock exchanges, Forex is traded over-the-counter (OTC), meaning there is no central exchange. Transactions happen electronically between banks, institutions, brokers, and retail traders. The market runs 24 hours a day, five days a week across major financial centers including Sydney, Tokyo, London, and New York.

Key Concepts You Must Understand

  • Currency Pairs: Traded in pairs like GBP/USD or EUR/USD.
  • Exchange Rate: The value of one currency relative to another.
  • Base & Quote Currency: The first currency is the base, the second is the quote.
  • OTC Market: No central exchange; trades occur electronically.
  • 24/5 Operation: Continuous global trading cycle.

How a Forex Trade Works

  1. Choose a currency pair.
  2. Predict if the base currency will rise or fall.
  3. Place a buy (long) or sell (short) order.
  4. Close the trade to realize profit or loss.

Major Market Participants

  • Central Banks
  • Commercial Banks
  • Hedge Funds
  • Multinational Corporations
  • Retail Traders

Why Forex is Traded

  • International Trade: Facilitates cross-border payments.
  • Hedging: Protects against currency risk.
  • Speculation: Profit from price movements.
  • Central Bank Policy: Currency stabilization and economic control.
  • Diversification: Portfolio balancing strategy.

Liquidity, Leverage & Volatility

Forex is the most liquid market globally. High liquidity results in tight spreads and efficient execution. However, leverage amplifies both profits and losses, making risk management essential.

Standard lot size equals 100,000 units of the base currency, though many brokers offer mini and micro lots for better risk control.

The Interbank Market

The interbank market is a network of global banks trading currencies directly with one another. It supports commercial transactions and speculative trading while enabling large institutions to hedge currency exposure.

Final Thoughts

Forex offers global opportunity, high liquidity, and flexible trading hours. Success depends on discipline, strong risk management, and continuous learning.
