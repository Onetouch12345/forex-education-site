What is Forex Trading?

Forex (Foreign Exchange) is the global marketplace where currencies are bought and sold. It is the largest financial market in the world, operating 24 hours a day during weekdays.

Traders aim to profit from changes in currency exchange rates such as EUR/USD, GBP/USD, and USD/JPY.

How the Forex Market Works

The forex market works through brokers that connect buyers and sellers. When you trade forex, you are simultaneously buying one currency and selling another.

  • Market runs 24 hours (Monday–Friday)
  • High liquidity
  • Uses leverage
  • Price influenced by economic news

Understanding Currency Pairs

Currency pairs are divided into major, minor, and exotic pairs. Major pairs like EUR/USD have the highest trading volume and tight spreads.

Risk Management – The Most Important Skill

Successful traders focus more on risk management than profit targets. Never risk more than 1-2% of your trading capital on a single trade.

  • Always use stop-loss
  • Manage position size
  • Avoid emotional trading

Common Beginner Mistakes

  • Overtrading
  • Using too much leverage
  • Ignoring risk management
  • Following signals blindly
