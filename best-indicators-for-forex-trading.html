What Are Forex Indicators?

Forex indicators are mathematical tools that help traders analyze price movement in financial markets. They are used to observe trend direction, momentum strength and potential reversal zones.

Indicators do not guarantee profit. They are only tools that help traders make decisions. Successful trading requires risk management and practice.

2. Relative Strength Index (RSI) – Momentum Measurement

RSI measures market momentum and helps identify overbought and oversold conditions.

  • RSI value above 70 may show strong buying pressure but also possible reversal risk
  • RSI value below 30 may show strong selling pressure but possible upward correction
  • Best used with trend confirmation tools

3. MACD – Momentum and Trend Change Detection

MACD is used to identify changes in market momentum. It compares two moving averages and generates crossover signals.

  • Signal line crossover may indicate trend change
  • Histogram expansion shows momentum strength
  • Popular among swing traders

4. Support and Resistance Levels

Support and resistance are price zones where market behavior may change. These levels are based on historical buying and selling activity.

  • Support zone is possible buying area
  • Resistance zone is possible selling area
  • Breakout movement can occur when price crosses these zones
